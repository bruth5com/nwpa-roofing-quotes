Roofing Built for Working Farms and Rural Property

Farms and rural properties put different demands on a roof than a house does. Wider clear spans, more exposure to open wind, and the need to protect equipment, hay, or livestock underneath all change what a good roofing job looks like out here.

What's Included

  • Pole barn and post-frame roofing installation and repair
  • Garage and outbuilding roofing
  • Metal panel roofing sized for large clear-span structures
  • Ventilation planning to manage moisture around stored equipment or livestock
  • Repairs for wind and snow-load damage common on open rural sites
  • Free on-site assessment of your existing structure

Why Farm Buildings Often Go Metal

  • Long lifespan with low maintenance
  • Handles heavy snow loads on wide, low-pitch roofs
  • Resists the wear of exposure on open rural land
  • Lower cost per square foot on large structures than asphalt

Local note: Farmland across Venango, Crawford, Mercer and Butler Counties often sits fully exposed to wind and snow with nothing around to break it up. Buildings on open sites like these typically see harder wind and snow load than a sheltered residential lot.

How the Process Works

  • Free assessment of your barn, garage or outbuilding
  • Straightforward quote based on square footage and structure type
  • Scheduling built around your farm's working season
  • Cleanup so equipment access isn't blocked longer than necessary
