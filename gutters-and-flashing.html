The Part of Your Roof Most People Overlook

Gutters and flashing rarely get attention until something goes wrong, but small failures here cause some of the biggest problems: foundation damage, siding rot, and ice dams that back water up under your shingles. Keeping water moving away from your home is one of the most cost-effective things you can do for it.

What's Included

  • Seamless gutter installation sized to your roof's water volume
  • Downspout placement to route water away from the foundation
  • Chimney and vent-pipe flashing replacement
  • Valley flashing repair where two roof planes meet
  • Roof ventilation checks to reduce attic moisture and ice dams
  • Gutter guards available on request

Signs You Need Gutter or Flashing Work

  • Water pooling near your foundation after rain
  • Gutters pulling away from the fascia board
  • Rust spots or visible gaps in flashing
  • Ice dams forming along the eaves each winter
  • Water stains on interior walls near a chimney

Local note: Pennsylvania's freeze-thaw cycles push water behind poorly sealed flashing and into ice dams along the eaves. Good drainage matters more here than it does in milder climates, and it's a lot cheaper to fix now than after it causes interior damage.

Common Questions

How often should gutters be cleaned or inspected?

Most Western PA homes benefit from a check twice a year, once in late fall before winter and once in spring after snow melt.

Can bad flashing really cause a roof leak on its own?

Yes. Flashing failures around chimneys and vents are one of the most common causes of roof leaks, even when the shingles themselves are in good shape.
