Why More NW Pennsylvania Homeowners Are Going Metal

Metal roofing is gaining ground across Western Pennsylvania for one simple reason: it sheds snow instead of holding it. Standing seam and exposed-fastener metal panels can last 40 to 70 years, resist fire, and handle the steep pitches common on farmhouses, camps and rural homes far longer than asphalt shingles.

What's Included in a Metal Roof Installation

  • Standing seam or exposed-fastener panel options
  • Full tear-off, or install over existing roofing where code allows
  • Proper underlayment and ventilation for condensation control
  • Trim, flashing and ridge cap finishing
  • Color options to match or complement your home
  • Manufacturer warranty paperwork provided

Is Metal Roofing the Right Fit?

Metal Is a Strong Fit If

  • Farmhouse, barn, camp or cabin roof
  • You want 40+ years before replacing again
  • Heavy snow load area with a steep pitch
  • Long-term cost per year matters more than upfront price

Asphalt Might Make More Sense If

  • You have a tighter upfront budget
  • You're selling the home in the next few years
  • The roofline has many valleys and penetrations

Local note: Warren, Forest and Clarion Counties see some of the heaviest lake-effect snow bands in the region. A metal roof's ability to shed snow on its own reduces the ice dam risk that plagues asphalt roofs along the eaves every winter.

Common Questions

Is metal roofing noisy in the rain?

With proper solid decking and underlayment, a metal roof is no louder than asphalt shingles inside a normally insulated home.

Can metal roofing go over my existing shingles?

In many cases yes, depending on local code and the condition of your roof deck. Your contractor will confirm this during the free inspection.
