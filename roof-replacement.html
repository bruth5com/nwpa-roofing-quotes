When Replacement Makes More Sense Than Another Repair

Most asphalt shingle roofs in Pennsylvania last 20 to 25 years under normal conditions. If your roof is getting up there in age, or a repair would only patch one more problem before the next one shows up, a full replacement is often the smarter long-term investment than repeated repairs.

What's Included in a Full Roof Replacement

  • Full tear-off of old shingles down to the deck
  • Inspection and repair of any damaged decking
  • New underlayment and ice-and-water shield
  • Manufacturer-rated asphalt shingle installation
  • Flashing replaced around chimneys and vents
  • Job-site cleanup and magnetic nail sweep
  • A clear, written estimate before any work begins

Repair or Replace? Quick Signs to Watch For

Repair Usually Makes Sense

  • Roof is under 15 years old
  • Damage is isolated to one section
  • Only a few shingles are affected
  • The roof deck is still solid

Replacement Is the Better Investment

  • Roof is 20+ years old
  • Widespread shingle failure
  • Leaks showing up in multiple rooms
  • Repairs keep coming back every season

Local note: Western PA winters age roofs faster than milder climates. Heavy snow loads and freeze-thaw cycles put extra stress on aging shingles, so a 15-year-old roof here may be closer to end of life than the same roof in a warmer state.

How long does a full roof replacement take?

Most single-family homes are completed in 1 to 3 days depending on roof size and weather. Exposed decking can't sit uncovered overnight in wet conditions, so your contractor will plan around the forecast.
